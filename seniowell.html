UX/UI Design

SenioWell - Senior Workout Mobile Application

PROJECT OVERVIEW

SenioWell is a workout application designed to provide simple exercises for seniors to prevent further stages of osteoporosis and arthritis. In this academic project, I collaborated with a team of two to develop a complex and interactive user interface. This involved research to identify suitable exercises and creating prototypes to visualize the app's interactive features

ROLE UX/UI Designer
TEAM Harshil, Mustafa
TIMELINE Fall 2023, 7 weeks
TOOLS Figma, ProtoPie
SKILLS UX researching, Interaction design, Wireframing, Prototyping

RESEARCH

Personas

To determine the types of exercises to implement in the app, I decided to create personas to show potential objectives and tasks that seniors may have when engaging with the app.

Userflow

To create a user flow, I need to first understand the user's goal and pain point when they exercise by analyzing the personas. Then, I created relevant features and all the necessary actions the seniors need to accomplish their goal. I did this to ensure all team members have a shared understanding of how we visualized our intended user journey.

DESIGN

Sketches - Home page, Exercise page, Favorite page

When I was designing the homepage, I found it challenging for seniors to determine which exercise workouts they should do. To solve this problem, I incorporated a recommendation workout section on the homepage, tailored to their medical conditions and preferred difficulty levels as input by the seniors. Furthermore, to simplify access for seniors who wish to repeat the same workout every few days without navigating through the exercise program, I introduced a 'Favorite' feature. This feature reduces cognitive effort and enhances the user-friendliness of our app.

Storyboard

After conducting research and discovering that seniors required ample rest after each exercise, I decided to provide a longer rest period, with the option for seniors to extend this period if desired. Additionally, we utilized short videos to visually demonstrate exercises, as seniors often found it easier to understand visual information. Furthermore, I implemented a congratulatory message feature to motivate seniors to engage in workouts more frequently.

Initial Prototype

Increasing Sign-Up Efficiency

I integrated a scrolling animation effect into the 'finish' button, allowing seniors to input their remaining information seamlessly. As a result, this ensured a smoother and quicker sign-up process, as seniors no longer need to manually scroll down to complete their profile, thereby enhancing their overall experience.

Enhancing Visual Clarity

I implemented a new color for the bookmark icon to signify when a workout has been bookmarked. This visual cue made it easier for users to identify which workouts they've saved for future reference.

Catering to Senior

I removed the resting period timer to reduce the number of steps required for the workout completion process. This granted users more flexibility in managing their rest periods between exercises, ensuring they're adequately prepared for their next exercise.

Refinement

Tailoring to Target Audience

I adjusted our workout program to better suit seniors dealing with osteoporosis or arthritis. By emphasizing pilates, yoga, and cardio exercises, my updated approach ensures a more tailored fitness regimen for their specific need.

Optimizing the Allergy Input Field

I enhanced the text area where users can specify any allergies they may have. This improvement ensures that users can readily identify it as a designated area for inputting allergies and understand the purpose behind its implementation.

Final Prototype

Simplifying the Animation

By removing the scrolling effect, seniors found it easier to input their personal information since it eliminated distraction.

Using Evidence-Based Senior-Friendly Exercises

Seniors recovered faster and safely because the exercises selected were proven to be suitable for seniors dealing with osteoporosis and arthritis, based on reputable sources such as Healthline and Harvard Health.

Encouraging through Excercise Quote

Seniors found it easier to exercise more regularly, thanks to motivational quotes provided after completing each exercise.

REFLECTION

Thanks to this project, I have deepened my understanding of design principles, usability components, and UI guidelines. Furthermore, thanks to the YouTube tutorial, I have learned how to create a highly interactive prototype using ProtoPie for the first time. If I have more time, I would like to recruit seniors to test my app and use the think-aloud method to understand their decisions. In addition, I would also like to incorporate a music feature to make it easier for seniors to exercise while enjoying.
